Lasagna

Description

This lasagna is reminiscent of the foods I grew up eating, and should be a staple in every good cook's home repertoire. The ingredients are either already in your pantry and freezer or they are readily available at any local grocer.

This dish will pair nicely with a full bodied red wine, for the drinkers in the house, that will allow the acidity of the tomato sauce to cut through while still highlighting the bold flavours. For a non-alcoholic option, an Italian soda or another carbonated beverage could be fun and a way to brighten up a heavier dish like lasagna. A meal in it's own regard, few bother putting anything else to the table outside of adding some extra carbs by way of a garlic, or even cheesy garlic bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 packet x lasagna noodles
  • 2 x 16oz cans of tomato sauce
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium yellow or sweet onion
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (mozerella recommended)
  • 1.5 tbsp olive oil
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 425F.
  2. Bring to boil a large pot of salted water, add lasagna noodles and stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook for 8-10 minutes or as per package directions.
  3. Meanwhile, heat a pan to medium heat, add the oil. Finely chop or dice the onions and add when oil is heated through.
  4. Add ground beef to onions and break apart using spatula or wooden spoon.
  5. When the meat has turned brown, add tomato sauce and heat through, set aside when heated.
  6. Strain noodles and lightly oil to prevent sticking.
  7. Inside of a casserole pan, or loaf pan for smaller portions, layer enough flat noodles to cover base, and proceed to layer with tomato and meat sauce until noodles are mostly covered.
  8. Alternate between layers of noodles and sauce until you have reached the top of your dish or the end of your ingredients.
  9. Top with shredded cheese mix.
  10. Bake in oven covered for 45 mins, then uncovered for 10-15 or until cheese becomes golden brown in colour.
  11. Remove from oven and let cool 15-20 minutes before cutting and serving.
