Buttercream

Description

Whether piping a child's birthday cupcakes, or dressing a wedding cake, a tried and tested buttercream can go a long way with any crowd. Try different colour and flavour combinations with this recipe to really wow your guests, or leave your family guessing which local delicatessen you stopped at for dessert.

A fairly easy recipe to put together, it is easy to incorporate into many desserts, including those that would be considered classic, and those more along the refined edge of the kitchen. See french macarons for a great recipe example!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tbsp jam or jelly of choice
  • Food colouring of choice

Directions

  1. Using a hand or stand mixer, whip butter until soft and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes. *Note, don't allow butter to become too warm at this stage.
  2. Incorporate the podwered sugar, 1/2 cup at a time until fully incorporated. A consistency slightly thicker than goal is wanted here, as the next stage will actually thin it slightly.
  3. Add in your jam or jelly of choice for flavouring (strawberry is a easy first use, but try to avoid the jams with large chunks of berries as it will distort the buttercream's final consistency.
  4. Add in food colouring and continue to mix until desired colour is reached.
